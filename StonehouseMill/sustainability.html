How We Work

Four Pillars of
Responsible Milling

Circular Materials

Every gram of fibre entering our mill has already lived a previous life. We source exclusively from certified post-consumer recycled streams — no virgin wood pulp, no exceptions. Our fibre procurement team audits suppliers annually against FSC chain-of-custody standards.

Water Stewardship

In 1989, we became one of the first UK mills to achieve full closed-loop water recycling. Today, 95% of our process water is recaptured, treated and reused within the same production cycle. Our discharge to the River Frome consistently exceeds Environment Agency standards.

Clean Energy

Since 2019, all electricity consumed at Lower Mills has been sourced from renewable tariffs. We are currently installing a 480kW rooftop solar array — expected to offset 35% of our total energy consumption by Q3 2025. On-site heat recovery captures steam from drying cylinders to warm our offices and welfare facilities.

Zero Waste to Landfill

Our waste hierarchy is: reduce, reuse, compost, energy recovery — landfill is not on the list. Paper trim and broke from our converting lines re-enters the pulper. Sludge from our water treatment plant is composted. Plastic strapping from incoming deliveries is baled and collected for recycling.

The Road Ahead

Net Zero
by 2030

In our centenary year, we committed publicly to achieving net-zero Scope 1 and 2 emissions by 2030. This is not an offset-based target — it is a genuine reduction roadmap built on measurable milestones.

We publish an annual sustainability report available on request, covering energy intensity per tonne of output, water consumption, waste diversion rates, and supplier audit outcomes.
